Important definitions

Semiconductor dies:

The individual chips or dies that perform specific functions, such as processors, memory, or specialized components.

Interposer:

A silicon-based substrate that serves as a bridge between the semiconductor dies. It provides a platform for mounting and connecting the dies and contains the necessary interconnect structures, such as through-silicon vias (TSVs) or microbumps, to establish electrical connections between the dies and the interposer.

Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs):

TSVs are vertical electrical interconnects that pass through the thickness of the interposer. They provide a direct pathway for signals to pass between the different layers of the 2.5D package, enabling high-bandwidth communication and reducing signal propagation delays.

Substrate:

The substrate provides a foundation for the interposer and helps to mechanically support and protect the 2.5D package. It also facilitates the electrical connections between the interposer and the external system or PCB.
