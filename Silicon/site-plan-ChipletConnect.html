Overview

Purpose

Silicon disaggregation, also known as chiplets, refers to the process of breaking down a complex system-on-chip (SoC) into smaller individual chips or chiplets that can be independently designed, manufactured, and interconnected. This approach enables greater flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness in semiconductor design, allowing for easier integration of specialized components and faster time-to-market. Advanced packaging techniques, such as 2.5D and 3D integration, complement silicon disaggregation by enabling efficient interconnection and stacking of chiplets, enhancing performance, power efficiency, and reducing form factor in high-performance computing and mobile devices.
